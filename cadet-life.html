Cadet Life: A Journey of Faith, Discipline, and Service

Cadet life at The Salvation Army Caribbean Territorial Training College is structured and centered on spiritual growth, discipline, and preparation for ministry.

  1. Spiritual and Academic Training
    • Cadets are trained to become Salvation Army officers with a focus on knowing God, understanding their mission, and personal growth in faith and leadership.
    • Spiritual formation is emphasized through private devotions, chapel services, spiritual days, and specific seminars like the Holiness Seminar and Prayer Seminar.
    • Academic training includes Bible studies, doctrinal teachings, and practical skills like public speaking, pastoral care, and financial management.
  2. Community Living
    • Cadets live in a shared environment where respect, cooperation, and adherence to guidelines are emphasized.
    • Pastoral visitation, including hospital and home league meetings.
    • Rules for personal conduct, appearance, and communal behavior are strictly enforced to maintain a harmonious living space.
  3. Daily Routine
    • Days are highly structured, starting with work sections in the morning, followed by meals, spiritual assemblies, academic classes, and field training activities.
    • Evenings are reserved for additional activities or free time, with lights out at 10:30 PM.
  4. Uniform and Personal Appearance
    • Cadets must adhere to strict uniform and appearance standards, reflecting the professional and disciplined nature of the training.
  5. Field Training
    • Cadets engage in practical ministry activities such as pastoral visitation, leading worship, hospital visits, and community outreach programs like War Cry sales and kettle collections during Christmas.
  6. Evaluation and Discipline
    • Cadets undergo regular evaluations to assess their performance and growth. Discipline is applied progressively if needed, ranging from private conversations to probation or termination for severe misconduct.

The goal is to prepare cadets to become effective leaders in The Salvation Army, equipped with knowledge, skills, and spiritual maturity. For more detailed insights, specific sections of the manual can be referenced.
