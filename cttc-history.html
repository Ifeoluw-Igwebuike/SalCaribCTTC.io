The CTTC History

The history of the Salvation Army Caribbean Territorial Training College is closely tied to the broader mission of the Salvation Army and its introduction to the Caribbean. The Salvation Army was founded in 1865 by British preacher William Booth, and its work began in the Caribbean in 1887 when a contingent led by Raglan Phillips was sent to Jamaica by the organization's International Headquarters.

The foundation for formal education within the Salvation Army in the Caribbean was laid in 1889, with the establishment of the first training school in Savanna-la-Mar, a town in western Jamaica. This school aimed to equip individuals not only with spiritual knowledge to share the Christian faith but also with practical education to sharpen their minds and instill the value of learning. The training school reflected a broader recognition of the need for a strong educational foundation to sustain the organization's efforts amidst the challenges of an increasingly technologically advanced world.

Today, the Salvation Army Caribbean Territorial Training College has evolved into a prominent educational institution in the region. It plays a significant role in shaping future leaders for the Salvation Army by providing training in areas such as theology, spiritual formation, ethics, management, and business. It operates in a culturally rich region known for its natural beauty and significant global contributions in sports, music, and other areas, reflecting the diverse and dynamic context in which the college functions.

Through its commitment to education, the Training College aligns with the principles of adult educational psychology, emphasizing the cognitive, emotional, and social development of adults in an educational setting. This foundation enables the Salvation Army to continue its mission of not only guiding individuals toward faith but also empowering them with knowledge and leadership skills.

The Salvation Army Mission Statement

The Salvation Army, an international movement, is an evangelical part of the universal Christian Church.
Its message is based on the Bible. Its ministry is motivated by the love of God. Its mission is to preach
the gospel of Jesus Christ and to meet human needs in His name without discrimination.
