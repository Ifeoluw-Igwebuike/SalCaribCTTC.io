Equipping Cadets for Practical Ministry: Field Training Program

The Field Training Programme at the Salvation Army Caribbean Territorial Training College is a key component of the training for cadets. It provides hands-on experience in ministry and community engagement, allowing cadets to apply classroom theory in real-world contexts. Here are the key aspects of the program:

  1. Objectives:
    • To expose cadets to Salvation Army ministry, including evangelism, pastoral care, corps management, preaching, teaching, and social interactions within communities.
    • To help cadets develop understanding, attitudes, and skills for Salvation Army service.
    • To engage cadets in proclaiming the Gospel and extending the Kingdom of God.
  2. Activities:
    • Brigades in city corps for Sunday meetings and mid-week ministry activities.
    • Pastoral visitation, including hospital and home league meetings.
    • Participation in open-air meetings and special events.
    • Annual evangelistic campaigns.
    • Selling "The War Cry" and participating in Christmas kettle collections.
  3. Brigades:
    • Cadets are divided into groups (brigades) assigned to corps.
    • Expected to engage in 6-8 hours of mid-week ministry activities.
  4. Preparation and Evaluation:
    • Cadets must submit sermon outlines, meeting plans, or Y.P. lesson materials to their Brigade Officer for review and corrections.
    • Field training activities are evaluated for learning outcomes.
  5. Integration with Studies:
    • Field training includes academic assignments during the summer, with grades contributing to relevant courses.

This program aims to prepare cadets for effective ministry by combining theoretical education with practical application in diverse settings.
