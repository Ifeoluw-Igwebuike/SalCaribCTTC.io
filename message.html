Dear Cadets

Welcome to The Salvation Army Caribbean Pastor's Training College!

It is with great joy and a profound sense of purpose that we embrace this new academic year under the guiding theme, "Building a Rich Legacy." This theme reflects the responsibility and privilege we have as leaders to honour the legacy of faith we have inherited, while forging a path of service and impact for future generations. The Keepers of the Covenant and Champions of the Mission are two wings of the same bird.

Scripture Focus:
"Therefore, my beloved brethren, be steadfast, immovable, always abounding in the work of the Lord, knowing that your labour is not in vain in the Lord." — 1 Corinthians 15:58 (NKJV)

Reflection:
Building a rich legacy requires more than knowledge and skills—it demands steadfast faith, unshakable hope, and a heart committed to Christ. The legacy you are building is not merely for today; it is a foundation for generations yet to come. By remaining steadfast in the Lord, your efforts, however challenging, will bear fruit for His Kingdom.
As you embark on this transformative journey, remember that you are not merely preparing for ministry—you are stepping into a sacred calling to lead, serve, and inspire. The training you receive here is designed to equip you not only with theological knowledge but also with the practical skills, spiritual depth, and Christ-centered vision needed to fulfill the mission of The Salvation Army.
Our curriculum and community life will challenge you to grow in faith, humility, and discipline. You will study the Word, engage in deep reflection, and gain insights into the diverse needs of the communities you will serve across the Caribbean and beyond. These years of preparation are a time to build a legacy rooted in faithfulness, integrity, and a passion for souls.
As we begin this session, I encourage you to:

  1. Embrace the Legacy: Learn from the rich history of The Salvation Army and those who have gone before you. Let their stories of sacrifice and service inspire your own commitment.
  2. Build in Unity: Foster relationships with your fellow cadets. Encourage and support one another as you grow together in the grace and knowledge of our Lord Jesus Christ.
  3. Prepare for the Future: Approach every lesson, training, and opportunity with an open heart and a willing spirit, knowing that you are being shaped for God’s glory.

May this session be a time of great discovery, transformation, and preparation for the incredible work God has called you to accomplish.In His Service,

Sincerely,
Major Vilece Thomas (MDiv)

The Salvation Army Mission Statement

The Salvation Army, an international movement, is an evangelical part of the universal Christian Church.
Its message is based on the Bible. Its ministry is motivated by the love of God. Its mission is to preach
the gospel of Jesus Christ and to meet human needs in His name without discrimination.
