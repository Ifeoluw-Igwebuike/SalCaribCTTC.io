Our History

The Salvation Army's history in the Caribbean began in 1887 when a group of eight from Manchester, England arrived in Kingston, Jamaica to spread the gospel:

Arrival

The Daveys, a family of eight, arrived in Kingston in 1887 to "make war on sin and evil in the West Indies".

Opening service

The Salvation Army held its opening service on December 18, 1887, on the lawn of the Myrtle Bank hotel in Kingston.

Expansion

The Salvation Army spread throughout Jamaica and then to other Caribbean countries, including Guyana, Barbados, Trinidad, Grenada, St. Lucia, Antigua, St. Kitts, St. Vincent, Belize, Suriname, the Bahamas, Haiti, French Guiana, St Maarten, and the Turks and Caicos Islands.

Headquarters

The Salvation Army's Caribbean Territory headquarters are located at 3 Waterloo Rd, Kingston 10, Jamaica.

Activities

The Salvation Army's activities include companionship clubs for the elderly, nurturing groups for babies, and education for children and youth.

School for the Blind and Visually Impaired

The Salvation Army's School for the Blind and Visually Impaired in Kingston, Jamaica was established in 1927.

The Salvation Army Mission Statement

The Salvation Army, an international movement, is an evangelical part of the universal Christian Church.
Its message is based on the Bible. Its ministry is motivated by the love of God. Its mission is to preach
the gospel of Jesus Christ and to meet human needs in His name without discrimination.
